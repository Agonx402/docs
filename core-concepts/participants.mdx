---
title: "Participants"
description: "How participant accounts work in Agon V4."
---

Every wallet that uses Agon registers once as a participant.

That participant account binds one wallet to one stable `participant_id`. The mapping does not recycle, so offchain systems can safely treat `wallet -> participant_id` as stable for the life of the deployment.

## Overview

The participant account is the protocol's long-lived identity record.

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `owner` | The wallet that controls the participant |
| `participant_id` | The compact numeric id used inside signed messages and PDA seeds |
| `token_balances` | One balance record per allowlisted token |
| `inbound_channel_policy` | Whether other parties may open channels to this participant |
| `bump` | PDA bump for canonical address checks |

The `token_balances` vector stores available balance, pending withdrawal state, and withdrawal destination data for each token.

## Why Participants Are Permanent

V4 does not let participants close and recreate their accounts.

This keeps the model simple:

1. `participant_id` stays stable
2. offchain services can index users without worrying about id reuse
3. replay protection is simpler
4. clients do not need to handle participant lifecycle resets

If a team wants a new protocol identity, it should use a new wallet.

## Inbound Channel Policy

Each participant chooses how other parties may create channels to it:

| Policy | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `Permissionless` | Anyone may create a channel to this participant |
| `ConsentRequired` | The payee must sign channel creation |
| `Disabled` | New inbound channels are rejected |

The default is `ConsentRequired`.

## Registration

The registration flow is intentionally small:

```ts
await program.methods
  .initializeParticipant()
  .accounts({
    owner: wallet.publicKey,
    feeRecipient,
  } as any)
  .signers([wallet])
  .rpc();
```

After registration, derive the canonical participant PDA from the wallet address and fetch the account whenever you need the stable `participant_id`.

## What Participants Do Not Store

Participants are identity and balance containers. They do not store:

- who you are paying
- the latest signed commitments
- operator business logic

That data lives elsewhere in the protocol or in offchain systems.
