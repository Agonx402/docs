title: "Cooperation, Trust, And Collateral"
description: "How Agon lets different payment relationships choose different safety and efficiency tradeoffs."
---

Agon does not force every payment relationship into the same shape.

Some teams want guaranteed payment channels with locked funds. Others are comfortable with partial lockup or periodic settlement, which makes the relationship trust-minimized instead. Many real integrations sit somewhere in the middle.

## Overview

Agon gives integrations three main levers:

### Locked funds

You can lock part of a payer's balance to a specific lane. That gives the payee stronger protection because that amount is reserved for the relationship.

### Settlement frequency

You can settle every call, every few calls, or much later. More frequent settlement means less timing risk. Less frequent settlement means better compression.

### Operational trust

You can settle directly, or you can add operator services with `authorized_settler` for batching, compliance, retries, or payment operations.

## What "Trust" Means Here

In Agon, trust does not mean an operator holds balances in a private database. Balances still live in protocol state.

It usually means:

- how long payments stay unsettled
- how much locked balance the payee expects
- whether an operator helps with submission or monitoring

## Common Examples

An internal service paying another internal service might settle infrequently and use little or no locked balance.

A public API charging unknown users might require locked funds before serving requests.

A high-volume provider with known customers might rely on periodic settlement and only lock part of expected usage.

All three relationships can use the same protocol. They simply choose different points on the safety-versus-efficiency spectrum.
