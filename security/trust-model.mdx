---
title: "Trust Model"
description: "What Agon guarantees onchain, and what still depends on the relationship between participants."
---

Agon is non-custodial, but it is not based on the idea that every machine-payment relationship should look fully trustless at every moment.

That distinction is important.

## What The Protocol Guarantees

Agon guarantees these things onchain:

1. balances live in protocol state, not in an operator database
2. only allowlisted tokens can be used
3. signed settlement messages are checked against the deployment message domain
4. lanes and participants are checked against canonical PDAs
5. settlement only moves cumulative state forward
6. withdrawals, lane unlocks, and signer rotations follow the configured timelocks

If a valid settlement message is submitted with the right accounts, the protocol enforces it according to those rules.

## What Still Depends On The Relationship

Agon does not remove all real-world tradeoffs between participants.

For example:

- one payee may require heavy lane locking
- another may accept periodic settlement and less locked balance
- one integration may settle every few seconds
- another may settle in larger batches
- one team may run settlement directly
- another may use an operator through `authorized_settler`

Those are product and relationship choices built on top of the same protocol.

## What "Trust" Usually Means In Agon

In most Agon integrations, trust means one or more of these things:

1. how much unsettled usage a payee is willing to tolerate
2. how much locked balance a payer is willing to dedicate to one lane
3. how much the parties rely on offchain monitoring, retries, and operator tooling

It does **not** mean the user has handed custody of protocol balances to a centralized ledger.

## Operators And Delegated Submission

Agon supports optional operator layers on top through `authorized_settler`.

That lets a team delegate submission work such as:

- relaying
- batching
- retries
- invoicing
- compliance checks

But the current V4 design keeps the payee's direct settlement path. `authorized_settler` is additive, not exclusive. That is an intentional choice because it avoids turning normal unilateral settlement into an operator-gated escrow path.

## The Right Mental Model

The safest way to think about Agon is this:

Agon is a public settlement and clearing protocol for repeated machine-payment relationships.

It gives those relationships room to choose how much they want to pay in locked capital, settlement frequency, and operational overhead. The protocol makes those tradeoffs explicit instead of pretending that one payment model should fit every relationship.
